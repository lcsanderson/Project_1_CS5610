what's on my mind

Recently I've been thinking a lot about product design and life cycles. Specifically how people are afraid to use beautiful things for fear of damage (aesthically, or fiscally). They are afraid to drive their Ferrari for fear of denting it, or eating off their wedding china for fear of chipping it, or exposing their designer sofa for fear of cat scratches and stains. And don't get me wrong, I don't think we should necessarily aspire to damage; but I do think that there is beauty in using things that were designed and crafted with intent. And if a dent or a chip or a scratch befalls these things then may it simply immortalize a life lived.

This is really corny, but a product I've been thinking about in particular is the Birkin bag by Hermés. There are other –probably more rational– examples, but I have to say I personally think this is an equally underrated and extreme case. And it's on my mind at this particular moment. Anyway, the woman the bag was named for threw all of her shit in there and flung it around like it was indestructible. She taped things (humanitarian flyers!) to the outside, she beaded and studded it (!), it was always overflowing (papers, journals, books, pens, scraps, precious scripture, art!), and that bag spent equal time on the floor as it did in her hands. In many ways you couldn't really tell it was a Birkin given her tendencies and habits. But the reality of the Birkin is that it is a well engineered bag and maybe that is how it should be treated. Jane Birkin treated it like it was invincible because frankly it was designed to be. Now her bag is something of an icon with it's softened leather and marks and bruises, etcetera. But today these bags are locked on flourescent display and only ever exposed to elite closed door events. They've become symbols of the wealthy (or aspriring) and guarded as insurnace. In the world I choose to live in, that is nothing but a Tragedy and a crime against craft and intent and whim.
